19. What does the Incoterm 'CIF' (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) require the seller to do? (single_choice, medium)

   C) Pay only for inland transport
   D) Cover all costs up to final delivery to the buyer's door
   Answer: Pay for cost, insurance, and freight to bring goods to the named port of destination — with risk transferring once goods are loaded on the vessel at origin
   Explanation: Under CIF, the seller pays freight and insurance to the destination port, but risk transfers to the buyer at origin port loading. It is a 'shipment' contract, not a 'destination' contract like DAP.
